## GAPS / COULDN'T SOURCE (flagged, not invented)

1. **No disclosed $ value for current (2026) Nvidia or AMD HBM supply contracts.** Only the older 2024-era HBM3E advance payments (~$540–770M each to SK hynix and Micron) are quantified. HBM4 contract values are undisclosed; allocation %s are analyst estimates.
2. **AMD's HBM purchases are not separately quantified** anywhere found — AMD's memory demand is only visible indirectly via its ~11% CoWoS allocation. No standalone AMD→(HBM supplier) dollar figure exists publicly.
3. **Samsung HBM dollar revenue is not cleanly disclosed** (Samsung does not break out HBM); only revenue-share (~22%) and qualitative "sold out / +50% capacity" commentary.
4. **SK hynix HBM dollar figure (~$28B) is derived** from "HBM ≈42% of FY2025 revenue" — note one source frames the ~40% as share of *DRAM* sales vs another as share of *total* revenue, so treat ~$28B as Medium confidence ±.
5. **HBM market-size estimates diverge wildly for 2026** ($45B Goldman vs $54.6B BofA; some market-research firms cite far lower ~$9B using a narrower definition). Do not treat any single figure as authoritative.
6. **No clean breakout of the "AI-driven portion" of WFE** or of each equipment vendor's AI/datacenter revenue.** ASML/AMAT/LRCX/KLA report total revenue; they attribute growth to AI/HBM/advanced-packaging qualitatively but do not publish an "AI revenue" line item. TSMC→individual-vendor dollar splits are not disclosed.
7. **TSMC's exact AI-accelerator revenue line is not separately reported** — only "HPC ~58% of revenue" (a broader bucket) and "Nvidia ~19% of revenue." 
8. SEMI's own press-release page returned HTTP 403; SEMI figures above are relayed via reputable secondary outlets (Tom's Hardware, EE Times) citing SEMI directly.
9. Several supporting datapoints (CoWoS wafer allocations, foundry-allocation tables) come from analyst aggregators (Silicon Analysts, Morgan Stanley via social) rather than TSMC IR — marked Medium/Low accordingly.
