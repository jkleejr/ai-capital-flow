## Summary

**Biggest optics flows.** The dominant directional flow is the AI-buildout demand from NVIDIA
and hyperscalers funneling into the transceiver/component layer. NVIDIA alone is ~27.6% of
Fabrinet's $3.42B FY2025 revenue (~$1B+), and the same end-demand drives Coherent's networking
segment to **$3.42B FY2025 (+49%)** and Lumentum's cloud business **+67% YoY**. At the switch
layer, hyperscalers (Microsoft ~26%, plus Meta) feed Arista's **$9.0B FY2025** with a **$3.5B
AI-fabric target for 2026**. Credo's AEC business tripled to **~$1.3B FY2026**, but is extreme
in concentration — a single hyperscaler (widely reported as Amazon) was **~86% of revenue in
Q3 FY26**. The whole layer rides a market sizing of **~$16.5B (2025) → ~$26B (2026), +57%** for
AI optical transceivers, with silicon photonics now the mainstream 1.6T technology (~72% share),
and a structural shift toward co-packaged optics led by NVIDIA (Quantum-X/Spectrum-X Photonics,
~$4B commitment) and Broadcom (Tomahawk CPO).

**What I could not cleanly source / caveats.**
- **Credo customer names**: SEC filings disclose concentration percentages but do NOT name the
  customers. "Amazon as the ~86% customer" and Microsoft/Meta as the others come from press/
  analyst commentary, not primary disclosure — marked Med/Low confidence.
- **Arista Microsoft/Meta %**: Arista's own FY2025 press release does NOT break out customer-level
  revenue. The ">40% combined" and "Microsoft ~26%" figures are analyst/press estimates (Med).
- **NVIDIA "$4B optics" and Coherent "$23B opportunity"**: these are secondary-source (Futurum)
  framings of NVIDIA's CPO commitment / Coherent's addressable opportunity, not hard contracted
  revenue — directional, not realized cash. Marked Med.
- **CPO unit/dollar flows** (Broadcom "tens of thousands" of Bailly switches) are qualitative;
  no clean dollar magnitude disclosed.
- **Coherent latest annual**: most recent completed fiscal year is FY2025 ($5.81B, ended June
  2025); by mid-2026 the run-rate is higher (~$2.05B/qtr guided for Q4 FY26) but a full FY2026
  total was not yet reported as of the research date.
- Market-size figures vary by firm (TrendForce vs LightCounting vs others); I used the TrendForce
  $16.5B→$26B/+57% headline corroborated by multiple outlets.
